📘 User Guide — Capacity & Engineering Workbook v6.76
This workbook runs entirely in one browser page. Your working data stays in this browser unless you export and share a JSON backup.

Quick Start

  1. Enter organization details, product lines, departments, and checklist items on Setup.
  2. Generate the workbook. Regenerating intentionally clears downstream entries.
  3. Enter delivery quantities on Demand using annual, quarterly, or monthly resolution.
  4. Enter capacity, standard hours, efficiency, and optional demonstrated capacity.
  5. Configure phases and department assignments on Lead Times.
  6. Review Utilization, Gap Chart, Headcount, Recovery, and Executive Summary.
  7. Use Export regularly to create a portable backup.

Demand Resolution

ModeUse
AnnualLong-range totals. If no sub-annual schedule exists, calculations distribute annual demand evenly for period views.
QuarterlySeasonal planning by quarter. Each quarter is divided evenly when a monthly calculation is required.
MonthlyMost precise timing. Once a product/year has monthly entries, blank months mean zero.

Important

Demand is always entered at the delivery or ship date. Lead-time settings move capacity work earlier without changing the original forecast.

Lead Time and Spread

  • Start Week is how many weeks before delivery the phase begins.
  • End Week is where that phase finishes before delivery.
  • LT Adjust OFF: capacity is shown at delivery timing.
  • LT Adjust ON, Spread OFF: the full phase load is placed at its start-by month.
  • Spread ON: hours are distributed evenly across the phase window from Start Week to End Week.
  • A valid phase requires Start Week > End Week, values from 0–260, and a department assignment.

A phase can cross a calendar boundary. A four-week phase may therefore touch one or two months, but its total hours must remain unchanged.

Forecast Boundaries

Lead-time work may occur before the first forecast month. Those hours cannot be displayed inside the selected horizon. The Utilization tab warns when work is clipped.

Best practice

  • Start the forecast early enough to include the longest lead time.
  • Add a buffer year when planning long phases.
  • Resolve every red Lead Time warning before relying on capacity results.

How Capacity Is Calculated

Required hours = Demand × Standard Hours × (1 + Rework%) × Office Copy Factor.

Available hours use demonstrated capacity when supplied; otherwise Base Hours × Efficiency or OEE.

Tabs at a Glance

  • Base Capacity: theoretical or demonstrated available hours.
  • Std Hours & Inputs: routing hours, copy factors, floor space, and dwell.
  • Efficiency & OEE: availability losses, rework, and optional OEE.
  • Utilization: period load, capacity, utilization, and gap.
  • Gap Chart: demand versus capacity visualization.
  • Scenarios & Recovery: capacity scenarios and dated milestones.
  • Footprint / Headcount: facility and staffing requirements.
  • Action Log / Exec Summary: ownership and management output.

Saving and Sharing

  • Autosave uses browser storage after the workbook is generated.
  • Export downloads a JSON backup containing workbook data.
  • Import replaces the current workbook after validation and confirmation.
  • Only import files from people you trust.
  • Clearing saved data removes both workbook data and saved commentary from this browser.

Recommended: export after major planning sessions and before regenerating the workbook.

Footprint & Warehouse

  • Production space is measured in square feet.
  • Warehouse capacity defaults to cubic feet and may be changed to pallet positions by warehouse department.
  • Monthly demand drives WIP and inventory peaks; annual-only demand is evenly distributed across months.
  • Dwell Days are working days. Space requirements use the simultaneous product mix in each month.
  • Production and warehouse units are never added together.

Troubleshooting

  • Monthly totals look low: confirm whether blank months are intentionally zero.
  • Load moved earlier: check the department’s phase assignment and Start Week.
  • Summary and detail differ: refresh the page and confirm you are on v6.76 or later.
  • Footprint incomplete: enter both Dwell Days and Space/Unit for every product with demand.
  • Hours clipped: extend the forecast start earlier.
  • Autosave failed: export immediately; browser storage may be full.
  • Old page after deployment: reload or open the page with a fresh query string.

⏳ Lead Times — Phase Configuration v6.76

Define independent lead time phases per MLI. Assign each department to exactly one phase per MLI. The model places full demand at Delivery Date − Phase Start Week for that department's capacity calculation.
Complete Setup and generate the workbook first.
How it works: Each MLI has its own phases and every department is assigned to one phase. With Spread OFF, the full phase load is placed at the Start Week before delivery. With Spread ON, hours are distributed from Start Week through End Week. Ship-date demand is never modified. Invalid or missing assignments keep demand at delivery timing and raise an error.
