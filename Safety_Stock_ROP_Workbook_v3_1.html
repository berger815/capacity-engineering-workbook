Start Here — Build a Defensible Inventory Policy

This workbook separates calculation from approval. It chooses a model only when the inputs support it, exposes assumptions, and prevents invalid rows from quietly entering portfolio totals.

1. Establish policy2. Load parts3. Resolve alerts4. Export the record

1 — Setup

Record the project, supplier, owner, revision, and assumptions. These fields travel with the JSON record.

2 — Policy Settings

Select continuous or periodic review, CSL or fill rate, and the default variability assumption. Fill rate requires order quantity.

3 — Parts & Calculations

Enter demand, variability, lead time, and cost. Review every yellow assumption and red blocking error before using totals.

Known Limits & Approval Checks
Annual demand and annual σ must be on the same basis before BOM quantity is applied. Do not mix project demand, component demand, and shipment history.
Future-only mode is a planning estimate. A stated CV is still an assumption until it is validated against actual forecast error or demand history.
Blank lead-time σ assumes perfectly stable lead time and remains visible as a review warning.
CV alone cannot distinguish intermittent from lumpy demand. Use transaction history, non-zero demand frequency, and empirical back-testing for high-value decisions.
Policy Position Value is trigger/target × unit cost. It is an inventory-position proxy, not average on-hand working capital.
A method override requires a note. The workbook blocks an undocumented override from totals.
